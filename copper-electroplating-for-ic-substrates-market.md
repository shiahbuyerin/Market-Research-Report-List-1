<p><p>Copper electroplating for IC substrates involves depositing a thin layer of copper onto surfaces to enhance conductivity and improve performance. This process primarily includes two types: acid plating and alkaline plating. Acid plating utilizes acidic solutions, resulting in smooth and fine deposits, ideal for intricate designs. Alkaline plating, on the other hand, employs alkaline solutions, producing thicker layers with better adhesion properties, making it suitable for applications requiring robust conductivity. Both methods are crucial in semiconductor fabrication.</p></p>

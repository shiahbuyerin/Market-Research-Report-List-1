<p><p>The flavored milk market is divided into two primary types: long-life flavored milk and fresh flavored milk. Long-life flavored milk is subjected to ultra-high temperature processing, allowing it to be shelf-stable for extended periods without refrigeration. This makes it convenient for consumers seeking longevity and portability. In contrast, fresh flavored milk is pasteurized and typically requires refrigeration, offering a fresher taste and shorter shelf life. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences and dietary needs, emphasizing taste and convenience.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Low Frequency Cards</li><li>High Frequency Cards</li><li>Ultra-High Frequency Cards</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The proximity card market is categorized into three types based on frequency: Low-Frequency Cards (LF), High-Frequency Cards (HF), and Ultra-High-Frequency Cards (UHF). Low-Frequency Cards, operating at 125 kHz, are primarily used for access control and are less sensitive to interference. High-Frequency Cards, typically at 13.56 MHz, offer greater data capacity and are used in applications like ticketing and RFID. Ultra-High-Frequency Cards, ranging from 300 MHz to 3 GHz, enable long-range reading and are ideal for inventory management and vehicle tracking.</p></p>

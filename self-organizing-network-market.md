<p><p>Self-Organizing Networks (SON) enhance network management through automation. In the D-SON (Distributed SON) market, intelligence is decentralized, allowing individual nodes to optimize operations locally and improve overall network performance. C-SON (Centralized SON) relies on a central controller to manage network functions, enabling coordinated and holistic optimization across the entire system. The Hybrid SON market combines both approaches, leveraging centralized oversight while enabling distributed functionalities, thus balancing localized control and centralized efficiency for enhanced network adaptability and responsiveness.</p></p>
